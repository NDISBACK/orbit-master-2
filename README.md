# Orbit Master

Space is beautiful from a distance.

Up close, it is unforgiving.

You are the pilot of a small spacecraft drifting through a planetary system where every movement is governed by real gravitational forces. There are no straight paths and no easy shortcuts. Every planet pulls at your ship, every burst of thrust consumes precious fuel, and every decision affects the outcome of your mission.

Your objective is simple.

Collect the first set of stars scattered across the system. Once they have all been collected, locate the fuel station and refuel your ship. The fuel station will activate a final wave of stars across the map. Collect every remaining star and complete the mission in the shortest time possible.

The challenge is not simply reaching the stars.

The challenge is learning how to work with gravity instead of fighting against it.

A skilled pilot can use planetary gravity to slingshot around planets, conserve fuel, and discover faster routes. A careless pilot may waste fuel, lose momentum, and watch precious seconds slip away.

Every run becomes a race against both the clock and the laws of physics.

## Features

Real gravity based physics
Momentum driven spacecraft movement
Orbital mechanics and gravitational attraction
Fuel management system
Dynamic star collection objectives
Time based gameplay focused on speed and efficiency
Smooth camera system with movement prediction
Planetary navigation and orbital flight
End game statistics screen
Replayable challenge focused gameplay

## How To Play

Your goal is to complete the mission as quickly as possible.

### Phase One

Collect all stars that are initially present in the world.

### Phase Two

Travel to the fuel station and refuel your ship.

### Phase Three

A new set of stars will appear across the map.

Collect every remaining star to complete the mission.

### Failure Condition

If your fuel reaches zero before completing the mission, the game ends.

## Controls

W - Forward Thrust

S - Reverse Thrust

A - Rotate Left

D - Rotate Right

Space - Brake

## Tips

Do not constantly use thrust.

Planets can be used to change direction and gain speed through gravity.

The fastest route is often not a straight line.

Conserving fuel is just as important as collecting stars.

Learning how to enter and exit planetary orbits efficiently can dramatically improve your completion time.

## The Challenge

Anyone can fly through space.

The best pilots learn to master gravity.

Can you find the fastest route, use the least fuel, and achieve the best time?

There is only one way to find out.

Launch and begin your mission.
